What Is Mardi Gras?

"Mardi Gras is the last day of a 10-day celebration called Carnival. Carnival takes place just before Ash Wednesday, and it is traditionally when Christians use up all of their rich foods before they begin a 40-day period of fasting (eating less) leading up to Easter. New Orleans in Louisiana, USA, is known for its elaborate Carnival and Mardi Gras celebrations. In New Orleans, groups called “krewes” throw big parties and exciting public parades with colourful costumes and floats. In a tradition that began in 1881, souvenir doubloons (coins), beads, dolls, and other trinkets are thrown from the floats to the crowds. On Mardi Gras, the oldest and most famous parades take place. Although the most well-known celebrations happen in New Orleans, Mardi Gras celebrations are held all around the world."
